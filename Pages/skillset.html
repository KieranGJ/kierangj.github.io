Data Analytics Skill Set

Data analytics is one of my strongest skills as I find myself constantly thinking outside the box and finding correlations between variables that might not even be in the same dataset. This ability is something I believe I could utilize in a business to gain immense amounts of value where I believe others may not. Alternately I am comfortable with the four major types of data analytics.

Descriptive analytics allows me to take data from a dataset and tell you exactly what it says. For a business this means I can see what items are selling well over time or which ones need some work.

Diagnostic analysis allows me to go a step further and figure out why certain things are happening. For a business this means that I can figure out why certain items are selling while others are not.

Predictive analysis makes use of my data science background to predict future trends utilizing either business knowledge or machine learning. For a business this means I can tell you if an item will sell well before it has even lauched.

Prescriptive analysis is arguably the hardest but most valuable of the four. It makes use of my critical thinking skills to take the information gained from the previous three techniques and come up with a strategy involving what the next best course of action would be.

When it comes to communicating data I am more than capable of utilizing tools such as Microsoft Power BI or Python to visualise the data appropriately so everyone can understand. I am also used to writing lengthy reports which document each and every decision I make and why I make them.
